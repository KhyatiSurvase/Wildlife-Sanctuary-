NATIONAL WILDLIFE PARKS - EASTERN INDIA

Home to some of the most rare and endangered species of animals in the world, National Parks in East India are a joy to the wildlife enthusiast. Owing to a rich and diverse plant life and vegetation, these national parks also offer a wonderful experience to nature lovers. Two of the most visited national parks of East India are the Sunderbans National Park and the Kaziranga National Park, both of which are inscribed as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. Overflowing with tourists every years, these parks are a great tourist attraction. There are several Wildlife parks in eastern India, but the most wel knoown ones are Kaziranga Naional Park-Assam, Daying-Ering Wildlife sanctuary-Arunachal Pradesh, Dibru Saikhowa National Park-Assam, Sundarbans-West Bengal, Jaldapara Wildlife Sanctuary-West Bengal, Balpakaram National Park etc.

KAZIRANGA NATIONAL PARK

Kaziranga national park is a national park located in Assam,India.It is home to the highest density of tigers and is also recognized as an Important bird area. This park is home to animals like tigers, wild water buffalo's, wild boars, barasingha deer, golden langur, indian rhinoceros and many more magnificent creatures.Tourism in Kaziranga include activities like observing the wildlife,including birding. Guided tours by elephant or Jeep are available. Hiking is prohibited in the park to avoid potential animal-human conflicts.The park remains closed for visitors from !st may to october end due to monsoon rains.The best time to visit the park is from 1st november to 30th april.

DIBRU-SAIKHOOWA NATIONAL PARK

Dibru-Saikhowa national park is a national park located is Assam,India.The park is bounded by the Brahmaputra and Lohit Rivers in the north and Dibru river in the south. Originally the park was created to help conserve the habitat of the rare white-winged wood duck, the park us also home to other rare creatures such as water buffalo,black breasted parrotbill, tiger and capped langur. The other fauna that live there are the jungle cat, feral horses, small Indian civet, Gangatic dolphin,wild boar and reptiles like the monitor lizard, tutles and snakes are also found there. Best season to visit the park is from November to April.

DAYING-ERING WILDLIFE SANCTUARY

Daying-Ering Wildlife Sanctuary is a national park loocated in Arunachal Pradesh, India. It is spread over a cluster of river islands. A cruise through the Siang river is the only way to reach the sanctuary. One can reach there by cruising the Siang river. The unique ecosystem of this sanctuary has made it a natural habitat for different species of birds including migratory birds like cranes, wild ducks, storks, waterfowls, etc. which come from Siberia and Mongolia from September to February every year. The forest islands are home to hog deer, hispid hare and wild buffalo, sambars,elephants and other rainforest macro and micro fauna. The sanctuary is a veritable delight to amateur and professional ornithologits.
